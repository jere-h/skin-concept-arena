How battles become rankings

Every battle asks the same simple question, and your tap counts as one vote for the winner. Those votes power two things:

  1. Peak medals (additive). Once a concept has fought 5 battles, its share of wins earns a tier medal — Bronze, Silver, Gold, or Diamond. Your Locker keeps the best tier each pitch has ever reached: peaks only move up, never down, and they add points to your career rank.
  2. Studio popularity. The same votes build a win-rate leaderboard behind the Studio passphrase, which the design team uses to read community demand. Concepts with fewer than 5 battles are flagged “needs more votes” instead of ranked, and exact win-rates stay studio-only — creators and voters see medals, never numbers.

Match-ups favor the least-battled concepts so new pitches calibrate fast, and you are never shown your own. The pool also includes a few AI-scouted concepts, developed from real-world references — they battle blind here like every pitch and are fully labeled in the Studio.

How medals become rank

  1. Tier medals. Once a pitch has fought 5 battles, its share of wins earns a medal — Bronze, Silver, Gold, or Diamond. Your Locker keeps the best tier each pitch has ever reached; peaks only move up.
  2. Career points. Peak medals pay 10 / 20 / 40 / 70 points, every badge unlock pays 10, and each vote you cast pays 1 (up to 100). Points only ever add up — your rank can never drop.
  3. The ladder. Recruit → Apprentice (10) → Artisan (40) → Virtuoso (90) → Master (160) → Legend (250).
